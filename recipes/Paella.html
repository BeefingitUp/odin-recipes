Description

Few dishes capture the spirit of Spanish cuisine as vividly as paella. Born in Valencia’s sun-drenched fields, this iconic rice dish is a celebration of bold flavors, vibrant colors, and communal feasting. Fragrant saffron-infused rice forms the base, mingling with smoky paprika, tender meats, plump seafood, and seasonal vegetables—all cooked to perfection in a wide, shallow pan for that coveted socarrat (crispy bottom crust). Whether you prefer the coastal richness of paella de mariscos or the rustic heartiness of paella valenciana, each bite transports you to Spain’s lively markets and seaside gatherings. Bring the fiesta to your table with our authentic recipes, and savor the magic of paella—a dish meant to be shared with joy, laughter, and a generous pour of sangria. ¡Buen provecho!

Ingredients (6 serving portions)

  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 4 ounces Spanish chorizo, casing discarded and cut into 1/4-inch dice
  • 2 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves - cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 (12 ounce) package uncooked Arborio rice
  • 5 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 1 sprig fresh thyme
  • 1 pinch saffron
  • salt to taste
  • ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 squid, cleaned and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 tomatoes, seeded and chopped
  • 1/2 cup frozen green peas
  • 12 large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound mussels, cleaned and debearded
  • 1/4 cup chopped Italian flat leaf parsley
  • 8 slices lemon, for garnish

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in paella pan over medium heat. Add onion, garlic, and pepper; cook and stir for a few minutes. Add chorizo sausage, diced chicken, and rice; cook for 2 to 3 minutes. Stir in 3 1/2 cups stock, wine, thyme leaves, and saffron. Season with salt and pepper. Bring to the boil, and simmer for 15 minutes; stir occasionally.
  2. Taste the rice, and check to see if it is cooked. If the rice is uncooked, stir in 1/2 cup more stock. Continue cooking, stirring occasionally. Stir in additional stock if necessary: use up to 2 cups additional stock, 5 cups total. Cook until rice is done.
  3. Stir in squid, tomatoes, and peas. Cook for 2 minutes. Arrange prawns and mussels on top. Cover with foil, and leave for 3 to 5 minutes.
  4. Remove the foil, and scatter parsley over the food. Serve in paella pan, garnished with lemon wedges.
